The Cognizant interview process typically has two rounds:

  • Technical Round: Mostly resume-based. They'll ask about your projects, tech stack, and scenario-based questions. Know your resume inside out ‚Äî every line is fair game.
  • HR Round: Includes a few aptitude/logical questions followed by soft-skills assessment (communication, situational judgment).
Prepare 2‚Äì3 strong project explanations with clear problem ‚Üí solution ‚Üí outcome structure. They value clarity over complexity.

For on-campus drives:

  • All academic marksheets (10th, 12th, UG semester-wise)
  • Government ID proof ‚Äî Aadhaar or PAN card works
  • Passport-size photographs

For off-campus drives: Check your interview invitation email carefully ‚Äî it will list exactly what to bring. But always keep marksheets + any government ID as a backup.

Keep both original + photocopy of every document. Some venues need you to submit copies that you won't get back.

Week 1 is pure admin mode ‚Äî no actual training content yet:

  • Laptop collection and initial hardware setup
  • Corporate ID creation and system access configuration
  • Email, VPN, internal tools onboarding
  • HR policy briefings and compliance modules

Your Mentor, Trainer, and POC (Point of Contact) will be formally introduced from Week 2 onwards when actual domain training begins.

Use Week 1 to explore the internal knowledge base. Ask seniors small questions ‚Äî everyone is usually very welcoming in the first week.

Training runs for up to 3 months maximum, split roughly as:

  • Week 1: Admin, setup, no technical content
  • Weeks 2‚Äì12: Domain-specific technical training with assigned Mentor + Trainer
  • Regular assessments and possibly hands-on lab sessions

Engage actively with your Trainer. If you're confused, ask immediately ‚Äî the pace can be fast, especially for niche tools like DataStage.

Location is not guaranteed to match your preference. It depends on:

  • Your Project Manager's team location
  • Client requirements for the project
  • HR Manager's decision during allocation

You can express your preference, and some managers do try to accommodate ‚Äî but be prepared to relocate.

Start preparing practically ‚Äî scout PGs/accommodation options in the major CTS hubs (Chennai, Pune, Hyderabad, Bangalore) before allocation is final.

Once training completes, you receive an official project allocation mail that includes:

  • Project name and client details
  • Your Reporting Manager and team info
  • Work location and reporting date
  • Any pre-joining formalities (background check, client forms, etc.)

From that date, you officially become part of your project team and your actual Cognizant career begins.

Before reporting day, reach out to your manager via email ‚Äî introduce yourself and ask if there's anything to prepare. It creates a great first impression.

The number of rounds and the package differ based on your academic background:

Arts & Science background (like Rahul ‚Äî BS CS/BCA/BBA):

  • 3 rounds: Communication round ‚Üí Aptitude/Assessment ‚Üí Technical + HR
  • Package: ~2.8 LPA (starts as intern, converted to FTE after training)

Engineering background (BE/BTech):

  • 4 rounds: One additional technical round
  • Package: Higher than Arts & Science (typically 3.5‚Äì4+ LPA depending on batch)
Rahul is from an Arts & Science college (Sri Ramakrishna College) and cleared 3 rounds. Know your background type before comparing rounds/package with others!

Package for Analyst Trainee depends on your educational background:

  • Arts & Science (BCA/BBA/BSc/BS): ~2.8 LPA ‚Äî starts as intern with stipend, then converted to FTE after clearing training
  • Engineering (BE/BTech): Higher package (varies by batch and year)
  • After training completion: FTE salary kicks in. For Arts & Science backgrounds, it becomes ~3.12 LPA after confirmation

Whether you join as Intern ‚Üí FTE or directly as FTE, both go through the same training and assessment process. The only difference is salary vs stipend during training.

No accommodation facility is provided by CTS ‚Äî you'll need to find a PG or flat on your own. Budget for this when you're calculating your take-home.

After your offer letter, a BGV (Background Verification) link is sent by email. Here's what to do:

  • Upload marksheets (10th, 12th, all UG semesters), PAN card, Aadhaar, and a photo
  • If there's an Affidavit section ‚Äî check if it's mandatory (asterisk *). If you have a Provisional Certificate, you usually don't need to upload it
  • If affidavit is mandatory: download the sample from the portal, fill your details, get it signed by a Notary, then upload
  • If you don't have a document, tick the "Unavailable" checkbox and add a comment (e.g., "I have Provisional Certificate")
  • If something is uploaded incorrectly, they'll send a pending BGV mail ‚Äî they won't withdraw your offer

After BGV is accepted, you'll receive the DOJ (Date of Joining) and location mail, then the onboarding pass about 3 days before joining.

Upload everything in good quality. Blurry or cropped documents get rejected during verification.

On your Day 1, carry exactly what your onboarding agenda mail says. Typically:

  • ‚úÖ Onboarding pass ‚Äî download from the OneCog portal (you'll receive it ~3 days before DOJ, also via email)
  • ‚úÖ PAN card (original)
  • ‚úÖ Government photo ID (Aadhaar or passport)
  • ‚úÖ Offer letter copy
  • ‚ùå Do NOT bring your personal laptop ‚Äî CTS will provide you a company laptop

Day 1 will typically involve: ID verification, laptop collection, badge/ID card issue (dark blue for trainees), and induction sessions. No technical work on Day 1.

If you don't have a physical PAN/Aadhaar (e.g., address update in progress), carry e-PAN and e-Aadhaar and inform HR in advance. They may adjust ‚Äî or ask you to confirm with HR before joining day.

During training, there are 3 evaluation stages ‚Äî Assessment + 2 interviews:

  1. Assessment: Domain-focused (programming/theory). Conducted by an invigilator ‚Äî some are strict (no phone), others lenient. Chat with cohort friends who went before you about the questions!
  2. Interview 1 (Mid-training): Conducted by an SME (Subject Matter Expert ‚Äî a real employee from your domain). Based on training content so far. Duration: 15‚Äì45 min.
  3. Final Interview: Project-based. You'll be assigned a mini-project during training. Final SME interview is on the project + full training. This is the one that matters most.
